The Great Sand Dunes

The Great Sand Dunes National Park in southern Colorado is home to the tallest dunes in North America, some rising over 750 feet. Set against the backdrop of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains, this unique landscape blends desert, grasslands, and alpine forests. Visitors can hike, sled, or simply marvel at the ever-shifting sands shaped by wind and water. It’s a one-of-a-kind destination offering adventure, beauty, and a glimpse of nature’s incredible power.


  • Hiking the towering dunes – some over 750 feet tall
  • Sandboarding and sledding down the slopes
  • Medano Creek – seasonal flow perfect for wading and relaxing
  • Scenic drives through diverse landscapes
  • Stargazing under some of the darkest skies in Colorado
  • Wildlife viewing in grasslands and alpine forests
  • Backpacking and camping in remote areas
  • Ranger-led programs and educational experiences

The photo captures the sweeping beauty of the Great Sand Dunes, where golden waves of sand stretch endlessly toward the horizon. Rising dramatically against the rugged backdrop of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains, the dunes glow in the sunlight and reveal intricate patterns shaped by the wind. This striking contrast of towering peaks and rolling sands highlights the park’s unique landscape, inviting visitors to explore, reflect, and experience the wonder of nature’s artistry.
